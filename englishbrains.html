But why are so many of Americans struggling with reading their native language? Well...It's all about the human brain!



The Science of Reading has conducted research on the brain and how the brain acquires reading. Since reading is a "new" skill, the human brain has not had time to evolve structures and pathways that are adapted specifically for written language processing, which is why many people struggle to learn to read. Basically, our brains were not made to read English orthography, or the English writing system.

Proficient readers use all parts of the brain when they read by linking pathways from the back and midle areas to the temporal areas and beyond, where the brain processes meangs of words and connected language. The temporal lobes are responsible for language comprehension and connecting spoken words with their meaning. Humans have only been reading for around 5000 years, which is relatively a short period of time for human brains to evolve and adapt.



In addition, English brings extra challenges because it is a morphophonemic language -- meaning that spellings represent phonemes and morphemes. Because of its complexity of being a deep morphophonemic language system, English is one of the hardest languages to learn and read. (Sorry English language learners!üò©)
